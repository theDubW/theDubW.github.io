Basic Simulation

This simulation is extremely naive; any contact between an infected individual and a healthy individual results in infection.
An individual is cured if after being infected for 20 seconds they are not randomly chosen to die, assuming a 2.5% mortality rate.

As you can see, it does not take long to infect everybody, and although the death count is low due to the relatively small mortality rate,
The speed at which the virus spreads seems to be dependent most on interaction between individuals, and the number of individuals who each person
interacts with while they are sick.
